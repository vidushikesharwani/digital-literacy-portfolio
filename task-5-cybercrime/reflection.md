# Task 5 — Reflection

## What Surprised Me Most
Researching UPI payment fraud for this task was both 
eye-opening and alarming. I was most surprised to 
learn that fraudsters actively monitor public social 
media posts — particularly complaints about apps like 
Swiggy or Amazon — and contact victims within minutes 
pretending to be customer support representatives.

The speed and precision of these scams makes them 
extremely difficult to detect in the moment. What 
surprised me most was discovering that scanning a 
QR code can result in money being debited rather 
than credited — a misconception many students, 
including myself, were completely unaware of.

I also did not know that India has a dedicated 
National Cyber Crime Helpline (1930) and a reporting 
portal at cybercrime.gov.in — resources that every 
student should know about.

## One Habit I Will Personally Change
As a result of this research, I have decided to 
stop posting public complaints about apps and 
services on social media platforms. Fraudsters 
actively look for such posts to target victims.

I will also never scan a QR code from an unverified 
source, and I will always verify the identity of 
anyone claiming to be customer support before 
sharing any information.

As a Student Digital Ambassador, I will share 
these findings with my batchmates to help them 
stay safe online.
