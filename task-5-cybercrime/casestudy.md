# Case Study: UPI Payment Fraud — The Fake Customer Support Scam

## Overview
UPI (Unified Payments Interface) fraud is one of the most 
common cybercrimes targeting college students and young 
adults in India today.

## How It Happens : Step by Step

**Step 1:** The victim posts a complaint on social media 
about a food delivery or e-commerce app (e.g., Swiggy, 
Amazon, Flipkart).

**Step 2:** A fraudster, posing as a customer support 
representative, contacts the victim via WhatsApp or 
phone call within minutes.

**Step 3:** The fraudster asks the victim to "verify" 
their account by sharing a UPI PIN or scanning a 
QR code to "receive" a refund.

**Step 4:** The moment the victim scans the QR code 
or shares their PIN, money is instantly debited from 
their account instead of credited.

**Step 5:** The fraudster disconnects immediately and 
the victim has no way to recover the money.

## Who Is Targeted
College students, first-time UPI users and people 
who publicly post complaints on social media platforms 
like Twitter and Instagram.

## Consequences
- Immediate financial loss (cases range from ₹500 to 
  ₹2,00,000+)
- Emotional distress and loss of trust in digital payments
- Difficulty recovering funds as transactions are 
  instant and hard to reverse

## Source
